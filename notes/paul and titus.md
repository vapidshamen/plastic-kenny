# paul and titus

10:15 - it's possible that's when he makes one deal, however -- in acts 18, right after his 2nd evangelical tour (AD54), there's some strange scriptures here:

paul is promoting that jesus (of gamala) is messiah (18:5), but when the jews resisted that idea (18:6), paul shakes his garments off (similar to herod washing his hands clean of jesus' death) and says "that their blood is on their own heads" .
back then paul must have still thought that jesus of gamala was actually the messiah and was trying to promote that narrative (which then got rewritten to be the new narrative jesus).
saul's reaction to the jews rejecting jesus, as their messiah, he obviously gets angry and tells the jews that they're going to die because they rejected of jesus (of gamala) as the messiah. right afterward (18:7), paul goes to "meet the family of a certain Τιτίου/titus Ἰούστου/*justus* (the just) worshipping the God of the house adjoining the synagogue" -- which sounds a lot like titus, the son of vespasian (who interestingly enough would be about 14 at that time and according to all accounts was actually an unusually just emperor). 

I feel like this reaction is a formative part of saul "turning sides", because there's a literary link here: in acts 1:23, matthias is elected over joseph/barsabbas (who is also called *justus*). the other literary link is found in acts 15:23 where it says that "they stood up two: joseph nicknamed barsabbas (who was also called justus)". it seems obvious that justus = flavius, so "joseph/barsabbas/titus justus" = "josephus flavius".

therefore, I find it very likely that a deal with the "family of titus justus" (flavius) may have been the first "deal" that was made (potentially converting saul from promoting jesus as the future emperor to continuing to promote jesus, but this time with the intention of provoking a revolt which titus/vespasian can quell and then become emperor).

judas (who we know from the narrative "betrayed jesus") had to be replaced, and so when they elected matthias over joseph/saul/paul/joseph/barsabbas, it seems that provoked a similar reaction, which (if the betrayal of judas was leaving for india) might have provoked saul to go off and do his own thing.
