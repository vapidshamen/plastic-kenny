## theogony and eddesa
notably, in [hesiod's theogy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theogony), [urania/Οὐρανία](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urania) is one of the nine muses. to understand how important theogony is, let's just go over the synopsis of what happens:
1. the first four gods are created: first is chaos/chasm, following with gaia/soil/earth (greek loves to add innuendo). these will become the foundation of the eternal ones/gods who are at the top of the snowy peaks of olympus down to the dim tartarus (in deep chasam of earth/soil) and eros/desire (fairest amongst the eternal gods - who "unnerves the limbs and overcomes the mind and wise counsels of all gods and men). from chaos came fourth darkness and night -- and from their love union produced aether and day. earth/soil bore starry heaven/uranus (equal to herself) to cover herself and to be a ever-sure place for the gods to abide inside of. she/gaia brought fourth hills and graceless haunts of the goddess-nymphs (who dwell in the amongst/between the glens/hills). she bare also the fruitless deep pontus without sweet union of love.
